METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------
This analysis demonstrates that observed redshifts can be
decomposed into physically plausible TSM2.1 components:

  z_obs = (1 + z_refrac)(1 + z_doppler) - 1

Where:
  - z_refrac = k_TSM × (N_HI_galactic + N_cosmic(z))
  - z_doppler = sqrt((1+β)/(1-β)) - 1 (relativistic)
  - k_TSM = 5.10e-23 cm²

Given z_obs and galactic N_HI, we SOLVE FOR the required
bulk velocity. This is NOT a prediction - it's a decomposition.

======================================================================
PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION
======================================================================
The TSM2.1 model proposes that observed cosmological redshifts
arise from two distinct physical mechanisms:

1. REFRACTIVE SCATTERING (z_refrac)
   - Galactic HI screen: from HI4PI survey data
   - Cosmic HI baseline: scales as (1+z)^1.9
   - Photons scattered by neutral hydrogen undergo
     frequency shifts proportional to N_HI × k_TSM

2. RELATIVISTIC DOPPLER (z_doppler)
   - Bulk recession velocity of matter
   - Follows relativistic formula
   - β increases with z, approaching ~0.85 at z~10

The decomposition shows both components contribute
significantly, with Doppler dominating at higher z.
